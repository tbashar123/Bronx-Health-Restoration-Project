Mental Health: Drugs and alcohol common in The Bronx

Everyone experiences stress and anxiety some point in their life. When that occurs, some people in the Bronx go towards drugs and alcohol to relieve it. Drugs and Alcohol may make you feel good but the risks are DEADLY. These risks include many diseases, for example heart disease, which is the leading cause of death in the Bronx. Instead everyone should exercise when they feel stressed and anxious. When you exercise, your body releases feel-good brain chemicals that may ease depression (Neurotransmitters, Endorphins, and Endocannabinoids). It increases body temperature, which may have calming effects, and reducing immune system chemicals that can worsen depression.

Marijuana

Marijuana affects eyesight by causing hallucinations. 30% of marijuana users may develop problem use when started during teenage years. Has the potential of increasing anxiety with some users rather than relaxing it.

Marijuana Risks

Causes loss of focus, slowed motor coordination and organization of information.

Cocaine

A stimulant drug that may give the user a feeling of intense happiness, agitation, and loss of connection with reality. Causes addiction after one use.

Cocaine Risks

  • May cause infertility and reproductive damage.

  • Liver, kidney and lung damage

  • If taken in high doses it can cause sudden death through heart attacks and strokes.

    • Heroin

    An extremely addictive drug no matter the method of administration to the body because of the rush feeling of euphoria experienced by the user. It alters physical structure of the brain.

    Heroin Risks

  • Failure to self diagnose disease because heroin suppresses pain.

  • Lethal effects intensifies with combination of other narcotic drugs.

    • Tobacco

    A stimulant that contains nicotine. Tobacco was named world's single greatest cause of preventable death by the World Health Organization in 2008.

    Tobacco Risks

  • Lung, mouth, liver, esophagus and throat cancer

  • Heart Disease and stroke.

    • Methamphetamine

    A drug that affects the central nervous system that increases energy and attention. It also decreases appetite.

    Methamphetamine Risks

  • Damage to brain that causes similarities to Alzheimer's disease

  • Stroke and epilepsy

  • Malnutrition and weight loss

  • Depression

  • Destruction of tissue in the nose if inhaled through the nose.

  • Severe tooth decay

    • Alcohol

    Alcohol is a depressant drug that impairs movement, vision, and cognitive judgement.

    Alcohol Risks

  • Birth defects if consumed during pregnancy

  • Causes 60% fatal burn injuries, drownings, and homicides.

  • 50% of severe trauma injuries, drownings and homicides.

  • 40% of fatal motor vehicles crashes, suicides and fatal falls and sexual assaults
